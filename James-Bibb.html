James H. Bibb

While James Bibb wasn't as influencial as John Cartwright or James Clemens, Bibb was still very important during the birth of Madison and often worked along with James Clemens.

James Henry Bibb was born in 1826 to James Bibb senior and Sally Alford. James senior worked as a tax collector and assessor.

James Bibb junior was certainly impactful and one of the leading founders of Madison, but was most known for being a very successful businessman. He owned a storehouse on Main Street which was close to Sarah Clay's house, which would turn into the Clay House Museum.

Bibb's house is still standing today, known as the Bibb-Whatley house and built in 1867. The house one of the oldest houses in Madison and is a little west of the historical district. The Bibb-Whatley house is located at 11 Allen Street.

Fun Fact: James Bibb was related to the first two governors of Alabama, William Wyatt and Thomas Bibb. They are all descended from Benjamin Bibb, who lived in 1600s colonial Virginia.

Fun Fact: Bibb was one of the main people who decided to drop the "Station" in Madison Station and rename it to Madison.
